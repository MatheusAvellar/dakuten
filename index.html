Nonstandard Dakuten Catalog

This is a list of the earliest use of each nonstandard dakuten I could find.

Dakuten, ゛, are Japanese diacritics used alongside some characters – such as か (ka), さ (sa) and た (ta) – to change their consonant's sound – が (ga), ざ (za) and だ (da). However, in popular culture and informal contexts, dakuten are sometimes used with "disallowed" characters, such as あ (a).

Characters in the table below are shown in red links if they have been found; in black if they haven't been found; and in grey if they are standard use. Shown here is only Hiragana, though Katakana is sometimes used, even in examples in this page. You can read more about the writing system's quirks on Wikipedia.
